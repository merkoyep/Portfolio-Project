About Marco

I was born and raised in Richmond, BC where I aspired to pursue a career where I could make an impact in my community. Through high school, I wanted to become a secondary school history teacher, because of my passion with mentoring others, and my interest in historical studies.

To gain experience working with young people, I volunteered at City Centre Community Centre as a program assistant, and was hired as a program instructor when I was 15. I continued working part-time throughout high school and university, until 2018, where I was offered a full time position as a youth programmer at City Centre. I accepted the position and was able to complete my degree as a part-time student. Through this experience, I discovered my obsession with making an impact in the community as a youth worker.

In 2021, I graduated from UBC with a Bachelor of Arts in History with distinction. Concurrently, I was offered a position to work for the City of Richmond as a recreation programmer for City Centre Community Centre. I decided to continue my career in recreation, and attained a position as a Community Facilities Coordinator in 2022.

While my role focused on the supervision and leadership of community recreation programs, I found that I was incredibly interested in creating digital solutions to business processes, and troubleshooting issues that we had with software in our business operations. At this point in my recreation career, I had the choice to pursue a more managerial position, continue as a programmer, or capitalize on my newfound interest to pursue a career as a software engineer and continue driving impact through technology.

Today, I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Applied Computer Science at the Dominican University of California. Additionally, I am a Dev Degree Intern at Shopify, where I am developing skills as a software engineer through hands-on experience, self-learning, and theoretical foundations.
